The AWAKE Method

10 Foundations of Customer
Experience Excellence

These are the non-negotiable standards. The baseline that makes everything else possible. Before you talk about experience you must master these foundations.

01
Service Is the Starting Line
Service excellence is your license to play. CX begins only after your fundamentals are flawless the guest can never feel the friction of your process.
02
Emotion Is the New Currency
People forget details but remember how you made them feel. Every touchpoint is a chance to make someone feel special, respected, or inspired.
03
Hospitality Is Everyone's Job
From the lobby to the boardroom, everyone shapes the customer journey. Hospitality is a behavioral language that defines culture not a department.
04
Make It Personal, Not Polished
Perfection can be robotic. Authenticity is magnetic. Let people feel your humanity it's the one thing AI will never replace.
05
Anticipate, Don't React
Excellence happens when you see the need before it's expressed. Curiosity fuels anticipation it's how you make people feel known, not processed.
06
Empower the Front Line
The people closest to the customer have the greatest power to create magic if you let them. Give them permission to act, not just to follow scripts.
07
Ritualize Recognition
Every act of excellence deserves a spotlight. What you celebrate internally becomes what customers feel externally.
08
Turn Feedback Into Fuel
Feedback isn't criticism it's a mirror. Listen deeply. Respond humbly. Improve relentlessly.
09
Design Moments That Matter
Map your customer journey and identify emotional peaks. Then overdeliver exactly there that's how memories are made.
10
Make the Ordinary Feel Extraordinary
Unreasonable hospitality is about generosity of spirit not spending more, but caring more. Customer Experience isn't a project; it's a posture. When service becomes second nature, experience becomes your signature.

Smile In Your Voice The Phone Standard
Phone Calls

The phone is the most intimate remote channel your energy, tone, and presence are fully exposed. Before your words arrive, your attitude does. The AWAKE standard: a warm, confident, unhurried voice that makes every caller feel like your only call of the day.

01
Your Tone Arrives First
Smile before you pick up it changes the acoustic quality of your voice. Clients feel your energy before your words land.
02
Slow Down to Speed Up Trust
Rushing signals anxiety. Pace yourself deliberately speak at 80% of your natural speed. Confident people don't hurry. They arrive.
03
Listen to Understand, Not to Reply
Stay fully present until the client has finished. Pause before responding. Silence is not dead air it's respect made audible.
04
Use Their Name Intentionally
Once warmly at the opening, once at a key moment of connection. Never robotically. A name spoken with care is the most personal sound in a conversation.
05
End on a High Always
The last thing they hear is what they remember. Close with something specific and forward-facing. Give them something to look forward to, not a vague "I'll be in touch."
Do
Smile before answering it changes your tone
Use the client's name naturally
Pause after they finish speaking
Confirm next steps clearly before hanging up
Return missed calls within 30 minutes
Don't
Rush the call or multitask
Use scripted openers that sound robotic
Put clients on hold without asking
End a call with "I'll be in touch"
Answer while distracted or in noise
"People can hear a smile. They can also hear indifference. Make the choice before you pick up."

Conversational · Responsive · Human
WhatsApp

WhatsApp is the most personal business channel in the region and the most abused. Clients who give you their WhatsApp are giving you access to their personal space. That trust demands intention. The AWAKE standard: every message should feel like it came from a human who genuinely cares, not a broadcast tool.

01
Respond Within the Hour
Speed signals respect. A client who messages you is in a moment of engagement catching them in that window is worth ten cold follow-ups.
02
Write Like a Person, Not a Brand
Avoid formal, corporate language. WhatsApp is conversational. Be warm, clear, brief. Read your message aloud if it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.
03
Never Mass-Broadcast Unannounced
Unsolicited bulk messages destroy trust instantly. Every message should feel personal. If you use broadcast lists, segment carefully and personalize the opening.
04
Use Voice Notes Strategically
A voice note at the right moment especially for warm check-ins or complex information adds a human dimension text cannot. Your tone and care come through.
05
Always End With a Clear Next Step
Every conversation should close with clarity: what happens next, when, and who is responsible. Ambiguity breeds anxiety and anxiety kills trust.
Do
Use the client's name in your first message
Keep messages concise one topic at a time
Acknowledge before you answer
Use voice notes for warmth and nuance
Set expectations for your response times
Don't
Send walls of text or multiple messages in a row
Use WhatsApp for conflict or complaints
Send mass broadcasts without personalization
Leave clients on "read" without reason
Be overly formal match the medium
"Giving you their WhatsApp is an act of trust. Treat it like the privilege it is."
WhatsApp Excellence Scorecard

Strategic voice note use
Clarity · Professionalism · Care
Email

Email is your written brand. Every message is a mirror of your professionalism spelling mistakes, rushed replies, and generic templates signal neglect. The AWAKE standard: communication that builds anticipation and trust before the deal even begins.

01
Subject Lines That Earn the Open
Your subject line is a promise. Make it specific, valuable, and human. "Following up" is not a subject line it's a missed opportunity to re-engage.
02
One Email, One Purpose
Each email should have a single, clear intention. Multi-purpose emails get responded to in the least important part or not at all.
03
Personalize Beyond the Name
Reference something specific to this client a conversation, a preference, a milestone. Generic emails get deleted. Personal ones get saved and shared.
04
Acknowledge Within 4 Hours
If you can't answer fully, acknowledge receipt and give a timeline. Silence is never professional it's just invisible neglect.
05
End With Warmth and Clarity
Close every email with the next step and a genuinely warm sign-off. "Looking forward to connecting" beats "Regards" every single time.
Do
Proofread every email before sending
Use specific, compelling subject lines
Reference something personal to the client
Keep it concise respect their time
Always include a clear call to action
Don't
Use copy-paste templates without personalizing
Send "just checking in" with no value
Use ALL CAPS or excessive exclamation marks
Bury the key point at the bottom
Reply to old emails without acknowledging the delay
"Every message is a mirror of your professionalism. Make sure you like what's reflected."
Email Excellence Scorecard
Compelling subject line
Personal, specific opening
Clear single purpose
Response acknowledged within 4hrs
Warm, clear close
Consistency · Presence · Value
Digital Presence

Your digital presence Instagram, LinkedIn, your website is your storefront when you're not in the room. It speaks before you do. The AWAKE standard: every post, story, and profile element should communicate the same warmth, expertise, and intention you bring in person.

01
Show Up Consistently
You don't need to post every day, but you must show up regularly. Inconsistency signals unreliability. Create a rhythm and maintain it.
02
Give Before You Ask
Share insight, perspective, and value without expecting anything in return. The professionals who build the biggest audiences teach they don't just promote.
03
Reply to Every Comment and DM
Engagement is a conversation, not a broadcast. When someone takes the time to interact, they deserve a real, timely response. Loyalty is built one interaction at a time.
04
Let Your Expertise Lead
Don't just share listings or promotions share what you know. The AWAKE Method, CX principles, market intelligence: make your insight visible and valuable.
05
Your Profile Is Your First Impression
A professional photo, a clear bio, a consistent identity. Clients check your digital presence before they contact you. Make sure what they find reflects who you are at your best.
Do
Post with a consistent visual identity
Share insights and value not just listings
Reply to comments within 24 hours
Use stories to show the human behind the brand
Tag relevant locations and collaborators
Don't
Post inconsistently or disappear for weeks
Only promote give without an agenda
Ignore DMs or comments
Share low-quality or unbranded visuals
Copy others without adding your perspective
"Your digital presence speaks before you do. Make sure it says what you mean."
Digital Presence Scorecard

Brand consistency across platforms
First Impression · Presence · The Room
Walk-In & In-Person

The walk-in is the ultimate moment of truth no screen to hide behind, no time to craft the perfect response. The client sees you and decides within seconds. The AWAKE standard: full presence, commanding grace, and an environment that tells your story before you speak a word.

01
Stand Before They Reach You
The moment a client enters, stand. Walk towards them. This single act communicates: you matter, I was waiting for you. It costs nothing and signals everything.
02
Your Environment Is Your Message
A cluttered desk, a broken chair, a coffee-stained table these details speak before you do. The space you invite clients into declares your standards.
03
Offer Before They Ask
Water, a seat, a moment to settle anticipate basic needs before they become a request. Anticipation is the most luxurious form of service.
04
Make Eye Contact, Not Eye Control
Warm, confident eye contact communicates presence and trust. The goal is connection, not power. Soft eyes, open posture, open conversation.
05
Send Them Away With a Feeling
Walk every client to the door. Thank them by name. Give them the next step verbally and in writing. The last thing they experience should make them want to return.
Do
Stand and greet every client at the door
Offer refreshments before they're requested
Keep your space spotless and intentional
Use the client's name throughout the meeting
Walk them out never let them leave unescorted
Don't
Stay seated when a client enters
Check your phone during the meeting
Let noise or clutter compete for attention
Skip the personal follow-up after the visit
Rush them even when you're busy
"The walk-in is where everything you say online is either confirmed or contradicted."
